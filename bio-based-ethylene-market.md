<p><p>The bio-based ethylene market comprises three primary types: sugars, starch, and lignocellulosic biomass. Sugar-based ethylene is derived from fermentation of sugarcane or beet sugars, often leading to higher purity products. Starch-based ethylene is produced through the enzymatic conversion of starch from crops like corn or potatoes. Lignocellulosic biomass, sourced from agricultural residues and wood, offers a sustainable alternative, utilizing cellulosic materials to generate ethylene. These sources contribute to a greener ethylene production, reducing reliance on fossil fuels.</p></p>
