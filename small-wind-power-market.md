<p><ul><li>Horizontal Axis Wind Turbine</li><li>Vertical Axis Wind Turbine</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The small wind power market primarily consists of two types of wind turbines: Horizontal Axis Wind Turbines (HAWTs) and Vertical Axis Wind Turbines (VAWTs). HAWTs, characterized by their traditional design with blades rotating on a horizontal plane, are commonly used for residential and commercial applications due to their efficiency and higher energy output. Conversely, VAWTs have blades that rotate on a vertical axis, making them suitable for urban environments and areas with turbulent winds, as they can capture wind from any direction.</p></p>
