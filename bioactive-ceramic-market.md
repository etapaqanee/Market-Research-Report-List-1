<p><ul><li>Hydrothermal Method</li><li>Sintering Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The bioactive ceramic market is primarily categorized into two production methods: hydrothermal and sintering. The hydrothermal method involves high-pressure and temperature conditions to facilitate the crystallization of bioactive ceramics, resulting in materials with superior bioactivity and purity. In contrast, the sintering method uses elevated temperatures to compact and densify ceramic powders, enhancing mechanical strength and durability. Both methods cater to specific applications in biomedical implants, tissue engineering, and dental restoration, contributing to the growing demand in the healthcare industry.</p></p>
