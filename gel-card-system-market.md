Forward testing identifies the donor's blood group by mixing blood with known antibodies. Reverse testing checks for the presence of antibodies in the recipient's serum against known blood types. Cross-match tests ensure compatibility between donor and recipient blood. Other markets include prenatal testing, organ transplantation, and research applications that benefit from accurate, rapid, and reliable blood grouping and compatibility assessments.</p></p>
