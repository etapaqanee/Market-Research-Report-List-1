<p><p>The Human Papillomavirus (HPV) vaccine market is categorized into three main types: bivalent, quadrivalent, and nonavalent vaccines. Bivalent vaccines target two high-risk HPV types, primarily HPV 16 and 18, which are associated with cervical cancer. Quadrivalent vaccines expand coverage to four types, including HPV 6 and 11, which cause genital warts. Nonavalent vaccines offer the broadest protection, covering nine HPV types, aiming to prevent a larger proportion of HPV-related cancers and diseases, thereby enhancing public health outcomes.</p></p>
